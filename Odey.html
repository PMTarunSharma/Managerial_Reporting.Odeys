Director overview • Odéys

As Director General, my role is to position Odéys as the region’s trusted operating system for the sustainable construction transition in Nouvelle-Aquitaine: a neutral, credible platform that connects members and institutions, accelerates innovation from idea to pilots/financing, and proves impact through auditable indicators. I will protect Odéys’ territorial DNA (proximity, neutrality, collective intelligence) while strengthening fundamentals that make impact scalable: disciplined governance, a unified operating rhythm across sites, reliable data, clear documentation, and a financially resilient model.

Mission: Network + acceleration + measurable impact Method: Simple, synchronized, documented execution Promise: Credible results, not just activity

Leadership intent (2026–2028). If you entrust me with the direction of Odéys, my objective is to strengthen Odéys as the reference operating system for sustainable construction and planning in Nouvelle-Aquitaine: deeply territorial and human, while structurally robust clear governance, measurable impact, and a delivery model that is simple, synchronized, and well-documented for members, partners, funders, and our team.

North Star (2026–2028): three outcomes

  • Trusted regional network generating concrete cross-sector synergies (companies, collectivités, research/training, associations).
  • Neutral innovation accelerator moving projects from idea → structuring → financing/pilots faster, with confidentiality and rigor.
  • Credible sustainability driver proving outcomes through auditable indicators (energy, circularity, climate, industrialization, digitalization, health/comfort)—not only activity.

From activity to an integrated execution system

My first act as DG will be to review Odéys’ real workflows end-to-end across sites and missions, and formalize them into clear process maps (workflow/UML style). This will surface bottlenecks, duplicated work, unclear decision rights, and hidden costs. From there, we will build “One Odéys Operating System”: Shared standards where necessary, and local autonomy where proximity creates value. The goal is just not bureaucracy, but fewer tools, fewer unclear handoffs, better predictability, and more time for high-impact work.

Strategic priorities

  • Governance & synchronization: weekly ops sync, monthly performance review (one dashboard), quarterly Board review; clear RACI; stable and fair reporting.
  • Member value: standardized member journey (segmentation, service catalogue, clear contact points), improved responsiveness, visible value through outcomes.
  • Innovation acceleration: single intake/qualification pathway, confidentiality embedded, structured progression to partners/financing/pilots.
  • Impact measurement: small, defensible indicator set aligned with Odéys themes to strengthen credibility with members and institutions.
  • Digital backbone + documentation: one source of truth for member/project/event/training data + Odéys Playbook (SOPs, templates, decision logs); automation targeting repetitive work.

Economic model and targets

Protect Odéys’ independence with a balanced, predictable revenue mix (membership packaging/retention, mission-consistent paid services, structured partnerships and multi-year programs) and secure EBITDA through portfolio discipline, standardization/automation to reduce cost-to-serve, tool consolidation, and rolling 12-month forecasting.

Closing commitment

I will protect Odéys’ values—proximity, neutrality, collective intelligence—while building the execution system that enables scale: simple, synchronized, documented, measurable, and financially resilient.

Deep plan for 2026 to 2028. This section consolidates the execution system, OKRs/KPIs, digital backbone, Board pack, and the quarter-by-quarter delivery timeline.

Executive Summary

This execution plan strengthens Odéys as the regional reference operating system for sustainable construction and planning in Nouvelle-Aquitaine: human and territorial, yet structurally robust through clear governance, measurable impact, and a simple, synchronous, well-documented delivery model. It is designed to be directly usable for governance, funder reporting, and internal delivery.

What this deep plan includes: (i) KPI architecture + Board scorecard, (ii) OKR roadmap 2026–2028, (iii) minimal digital backbone + data sources, (iv) quarterly Board Pack template, and (v) Q1-2026 blueprint based on workflow/UML process review.

KPI Architecture (Board Scorecard)

A minimal, decision-oriented scorecard (12–15 KPIs) grouped into five families. Definitions are locked in the Playbook to ensure consistency.

KPI family What is measured (examples)
Operational Excellence (OE) Cycle time, Automation, Data quality, SLA adherence
Network & Member Value (NM) Retention, Engagement index, Satisfaction, Collaboration rate
Innovation Acceleration (IA) Qualification lead time, Next-milestone conversion, Throughput, Confidentiality compliance
Sustainability & Market Impact (SI) Outcome-indicator coverage, Adoption outcomes, Auditability of impact report
Financial Resilience (FR) Revenue diversification, Earned income growth, EBITDA safety band, Forecast accuracy & cash discipline

Cadence: weekly operational sync (OE), monthly performance review (OE/NM/IA/FR), quarterly Board review (all KPI families + decisions).

OKRs 2026–2028 (Board-ready)

Baselines are set during the Q1-2026 diagnostic (workflow/UML review + data audit).

2026 — Foundation

Objective 1: Synchronize governance and decision-making across 5 locations

  • KR1.1 Core workflows mapped and validated (workflow/UML): 100% core workflows
  • KR1.2 RACI approved and used: 100% processes with RACI
  • KR1.3 Cadence installed (weekly/monthly/quarterly packs): ≥90% attendance; 100% on-time packs

Objective 2: Build a single source of truth and Playbook v1

  • KR2.1 Data completeness & accuracy: ≥90% completeness; ≥95% accuracy
  • KR2.2 Playbook v1 published: ≥15 SOPs + templates + logs
  • KR2.3 Member request cycle time reduced: ≥30% reduction vs baseline

Objective 3: Automate recurring work and improve reliability

  • KR3.1 Admin automation rate: ≥60% automated; ≥40h/month saved
  • KR3.2 Events workflow automation + feedback: ≥40% feedback; ≥80% follow-up in 10 days
  • KR3.3 Funder reporting on-time: 100% on-time

Objective 4: Secure financial controls and EBITDA safety rules

  • KR4.1 Rolling 12-month forecast: forecast variance < 10% by Q4
  • KR4.2 Portfolio scoring applied: 100% new initiatives scored
  • KR4.3 Tool/vendor consolidation: savings target set Q2; achieved Q4
2027 — Scale

Objective 1: Increase member value, retention, and engagement

  • KR1.1 Retention/renewal improvement: +5 pts vs 2026
  • KR1.2 Satisfaction improvement: +10 pts (NPS or equivalent)
  • KR1.3 Collaboration rate increase: ≥15% increase

Objective 2: Make innovation support faster and more predictable

  • KR2.1 Intake → qualification time: ≤10 working days
  • KR2.2 Next-milestone conversion: ≥20% increase vs 2026
  • KR2.3 Confidentiality compliance: 100% for flagged projects

Objective 3: Prove sustainability outcomes (not only activity)

  • KR3.1 Outcome-indicator coverage: ≥80% coverage
  • KR3.2 Publish scorecard: 2 publications/year (mid-year + annual)
  • KR3.3 Adoption outcomes (QBDNA/best practices): targets set Q1; achieved Q4

Objective 4: Grow earned income and increase resilience

  • KR4.1 Service/program revenue growth: +15–25% or Board target
  • KR4.2 Dependency reduction (top source share): trend toward threshold
  • KR4.3 EBITDA within safety band: maintain within band
2028 — Reference

Objective 1: Establish Odéys as the regional benchmark for transition delivery

  • KR1.1 Annual impact report (auditable): published with evidence pack
  • KR1.2 Multi-year strategic partnerships: ≥15% increase vs 2027
  • KR1.3 Satisfaction consistency across sites: maintain or improve 2027

Objective 2: Operational maturity: scalable quality at lower cost-to-serve

  • KR2.1 Cost-to-serve reduction: ≥10–15% vs 2026
  • KR2.2 Data quality sustained: ≥95% completeness; ≥97% accuracy
  • KR2.3 Playbook v3 continuous improvement: each core process improved ≥2 times since 2026

Objective 3: Secure long-term financial independence and EBITDA safety

  • KR3.1 Revenue mix meets thresholds: thresholds respected
  • KR3.2 Earned income predictable share: target share achieved
  • KR3.3 EBITDA safety + operating buffer: buffer policy implemented

Digital Backbone & Data Sources (Implementation-ready)

Minimal stack with one system of record per domain; BI is read-only and fed from systems of record.

Domain (system of record) What it contains
Members & Partners (CRM) Orgs, contacts, segmentation, renewals, partnerships, interactions
Projects & Innovation Portfolio (PPM) Intake, qualification, stages, milestones, confidentiality, outcomes
Events & Training (Event/LMS) Registrations, attendance, surveys, certifications, follow-ups
Knowledge & Playbook (KB) SOPs, templates, decision logs, RACI, workflow/UML diagrams
Finance (Accounting) Revenue mix, earned income, forecasts, EBITDA, cash discipline
Dashboards (BI) Scorecard + quarterly Board pack (read-only)

Quarterly Board Pack (template)

Standard structure (4–6 pages) with explicit decisions requested at the end of each pack: Executive scorecard; OKR status; portfolio & pipeline; financial page; risk register.
